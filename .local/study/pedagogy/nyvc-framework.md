# NYVC Pedagogy Framework
## Justin Stoney - New York Vocal Coaching

### The Hardware-Software Stack

**HARDWARE (Physical Mechanisms):**
- Breath support & appoggio
- Vocal fold closure (adduction)
- Laryngeal positioning
- Resonance tuning (formant tracking)
- Articulator shaping (tongue, jaw, lips)

**SOFTWARE (Coordination Patterns):**
- Registration coordination (chest/mix/head)
- Dynamic control (crescendo/decrescendo)
- Vowel modification (covering)
- Style-specific coordination (belt, legit, mix, etc.)

### Core Principles

1. **Every voice is a instrument** — not a problem to fix
2. **Coordination over strength** — efficiency before power
3. **Style follows function** — technique serves expression
4. **The vocal tract is a filter** — source + filter = sound
5. **Registration is not range** — it's coordination, not "hitting notes"

### Common Misconceptions (Debunked)

| Myth | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Push harder for high notes" | Modify vowels, thin folds, adjust resonance |
| "Belt is dangerous" | Belt is a coordination, not a strain |
| "You're a tenor/soprano" | Range is coordination, not fixed identity |
| "More breath = more power" | Efficient closure > air pressure |
| "Mix voice is a secret" | It's just balanced registration |

### Exercise Categories

1. **Onset exercises** — clean starts, no breathiness, no glottal
2. **Sustain exercises** — steady tone, consistent airflow
3. **Scale exercises** — registration transitions, vowel modification
4. **Agility exercises** — runs, melisma, quick coordination changes
5. **Style exercises** — belt, mix, legit, twang, cry, etc.
6. **Resonance exercises** — forward placement, ring, "the cry"

### The "Fletcher" Scale (Vowel Modification)
- Low range: Open vowels (AH, OH)
- Mid range: Moderate modification (EH, UH)
- High range: Closed/modified vowels (EE, OO)
- The "Fletcher" is the point where modification becomes necessary

### Belt Coordination
- Thick vocal folds
- High laryngeal position (not extreme)
- "Cry" or "twang" resonance
- Chest-dominant but not "chest voice"
- Requires efficient closure, not pushing

### Mix Coordination
- Balanced fold thickness
- Neutral laryngeal position
- Neither chest nor head dominant
- The "sweet spot" for most contemporary singing
- Requires registration balance, not "blending"

### Practice Philosophy
- 15-20 minutes daily > 2 hours once a week
- Mirror work for visual feedback
- Recording for auditory feedback
- Slow practice for coordination building
- Apply to songs immediately (not just exercises)
