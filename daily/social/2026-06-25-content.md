## C) TWITTER/X THREAD (7 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Your voice isn't tired after 10 minutes of singing. It's tense.

Most vocal fatigue is a tension problem, not a stamina problem. Here are the 3 hidden patterns draining you — and a 90-second reset that fixes all of them:

**Tweet 2:**
Pattern #1: Jaw clamping.

As you sing higher, your jaw presses down — tightening the tongue root, constricting your throat. You feel it as "reaching" for notes. Your voice gets tighter the higher you go.

**Tweet 3:**
Pattern #2: Neck and shoulder bracing.

Unconscious tension that transmits into the larynx. You'll feel it as tightness at the front of your neck, a "choking" sensation, or a voice that feels squeezed out.

**Tweet 4:**
Pattern #3: Breath-holding / over-pressurizing.

Gripping your breath with your throat while trying to push it out. Your voice feels "strong" at first but fatigues fast — all power, no efficiency.

**Tweet 5:**
The 90-Second Reset:
① Jaw release — massage the hinge, let it hang (20 sec)
② Neck decompression — chin down, roll side to side (20 sec)
③ Shoulder drop — lift, hold, DROP ×3 (15 sec)
④ Lip trill — descending scale, check for flow (20 sec)
⑤ Hum check — keep the buzz in your lips (15 sec)

**Tweet 6:**
The rule I teach every student:
