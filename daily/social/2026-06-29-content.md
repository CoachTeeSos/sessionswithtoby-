## C) Twitter/X Thread (7 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Your voice doesn't fail on long phrases because you run out of air.

It fails because of pressure leaks you can't feel.

Here are the 3 leaks + a 30-second reset that fixes all of them 🧵

**Tweet 2 (First leak):**
Leak #1: The Jaw Clamp.

As you approach the end of a phrase, your jaw closes slightly. You can't feel it happening. But the resonance chamber just got smaller — and your tone collapses.

Fix: Let your jaw drop MORE as the phrase progresses. Counter-movement.

**Tweet 3 (Second leak):**
Leak #2: The Throat Squeeze.

When your lungs feel low, your throat tries to help by tightening. It creates turbulence, makes your vocal folds work harder, and you sound exhausted.

Fix: Your throat's job is to STAY OPEN. Air exits on its own. Don't regulate speed.

**Tweet 4 (Third leak):**
Leak #3: The Soft Palate Collapse.

The fleshy part at the roof of your mouth drops as you near the end of a phrase. Sound loses warmth. Projection dies. This is the one most coaches miss.

Fix: Maintain a subtle lift through the entire phrase.

**Tweet 5 (The reset intro):**
The Reset (do this NOW):

Step 1 — Confirming Sigh (10s)
Relax. Sigh out on "ahh." Feel your open throat. That's your baseline. That's what singing should feel like.

**Tweet 6 (Steps 2-3):**
Step 2 — Suspended Note (10s)
Same relaxed breath. Sustain a single note on "ahh." If tension builds, you're leaking pressure the wrong way.

Step 3 — Phrase Simulation (10s)
Sing a long phrase. At the LAST 30% of air, drop your jaw MORE. Lift your palate. Override the collapse reflex.

**Tweet 7 (CTA):**
Do this 3x.

By round 3, your voice stops "running out" at the same point. The air goes further because it stops leaking.
