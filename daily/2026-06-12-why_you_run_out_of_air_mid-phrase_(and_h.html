The Singer Who Couldn't Breath

He could hit any note in his range. His tone was warm, full, technically impressive. But every time he performed, his phrases would die halfway through. He'd run out of air right before the important word.

Your lungs are fine. Your vocal folds are leaking air.

The The ZH Exercise (2 minutes)

This is today's targeted exercise. Do it exactly as described.

1
Say 'ZH' — like the 's' in 'measure.' Sustain it: 'ZZZZHHH.' Feel the buzz in your mouth.
2
Put a voice behind it. 'VZHH' — like a very exaggerated 'V' into a voiced 'ZH.' Feel the vibration start.
3
Sing 'ZHee' on a scale: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1. The 'ZH' onset ensures clean fold closure before the note starts.
4
Now sing 'See' on the same scale — but keep the same efficient onset. Notice how less air escapes.
2 minutes · 3x daily for 1 week

Why This Works

This trains your vocal folds to close efficiently. You're not running out of air — you're spending it badly. Clean fold closure means less air waste.
