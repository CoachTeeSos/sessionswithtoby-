Why Your Voice Cracks (And How to Fix It)

Day 1 · Sessions with Toby

🎭 The Story

Sarah had been singing for 5 years. She could belt in the shower, hit most notes in her range, and even performed at a local open mic. But every time she tried to sing in front of others, her voice would crack on the high notes. She'd feel her throat tighten, her breath run out, and the embarrassment would wash over her.

"I thought I just wasn't talented enough," she told me during our first session. "Everyone else makes it look so easy."

Here's what I told Sarah: Your voice isn't broken. Your coordination is.

🔍 The Problem: Voice cracks and breaks in the passaggio

This is the #1 issue I see with singers. It's not about talent. It's about coordination — specifically, how your vocal folds, breath, and resonance work together.

Most singers try to push harder when they hit a wall. But that's like pressing the gas pedal harder when your car won't go faster. The problem isn't power — it's the gear you're in.

⚡ The Fix: The Cry Exercise

Try this right now. It takes 2 minutes.

Step 1: Think of a sad movie moment. Feel the "sob" in your chest.
Step 2: Say "Wah" (like a baby crying) on a comfortable pitch. Feel the cry-like quality.
Step 3: Sing "Wah" on a 5-tone scale: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1
Step 4: Notice how the high notes feel easier. That's your mix voice engaging.

⏱️ Time: 2 minutes · Do this 3x daily for a week

💡 Why This Works

The "cry" coordination engages your mix voice — the registration that lets you sing high notes without straining. It's not about pushing. It's about allowing.

When you feel the "sob," your vocal folds thin out naturally, your larynx stays neutral, and your resonance shifts forward. That's the coordination that professional singers use instinctively.
