## Quick Answer

To improve your singing voice: **fix your breathing first** (diaphragm, not chest), **reduce throat tension** (lip trills, humming), and **practice consistently** (10 min/day beats 2 hours once a week). Most singers have a technique problem, not a talent problem.

---

## Why Your Voice Isn't Improving

If you've been singing for months or years and sound the same, it's not because you lack talent. It's because you're practicing the wrong things.

The 3 most common issues:

1. **Singing from your throat** — Your throat should feel relaxed after singing. If it hurts, you're pushing.
2. **Chest breathing** — Shoulders going up = wrong. Stomach going out = right.
3. **No consistent routine** — 10 focused minutes daily beats 2 hours of random practice weekly.

> "Your voice is not the problem. Your technique is." — Toby Wina

## The 5 Exercises That Change Everything

### 1. The Lip Thill (2 minutes)
**What it fixes:** Throat tension, breath support, register connection

**How:**
1. Relax your lips
2. Blow air through them so they flutter (like a horse)
3. Slide from low to high on the flutter
4. 3 rounds, 5-note scales

**Why it works:** The lip trill forces your diaphragm to do the work. Your throat stays relaxed. It's physically impossible to strain while doing a proper lip trill.

### 2. The Hum Slide (2 minutes)
**What it fixes:** Register connection, forward placement

**How:**
1. Close your lips
2. Hum from your lowest comfortable note to your highest
3. Feel the vibration move from your chest to your face
4. Let it crack — that's your passaggio (transition point)
5. 5 reps

**Why it works:** Humming naturally places the sound forward. The slide connects your chest and head voice.

### 3. The Ng Exercise (2 minutes)
**What it fixes:** Thin tone, lack of resonance

**How:**
1. Say "sing" — hold the "ng" at the end
2. Feel the buzz in your nose and cheekbones
3. Hold for 5 seconds
4. 5 reps

**Why it works:** The "ng" position creates forward resonance. This is what makes your tone sound full instead of thin.

### 4. The Siren (2 minutes)
**What it fixes:** Range limitation, register breaks

**How:**
1. On "wee," slide from your lowest note to your highest
2. Let it crack — don't avoid the break
5. 5 reps

**Why it works:** The siren trains your voice to transition smoothly between registers. Avoiding the crack is what keeps most singers stuck.

### 5. Whisper Sing (2 minutes)
**What it fixes:** Breath control, tension habits

**How:**
1. Sing your favorite song at whisper volume
2. Focus on keeping your throat completely relaxed
3. One full song

**Why it works:** Whisper singing removes all the tension habits you've built up. It resets your voice.

## The 10-Minute Daily Routine

Do these in order, every day:

| Minute | Exercise | Focus |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Hum Slide | Warm up, connect registers |
| 3-4 | Lip Trill | Breath support, reduce tension |
| 5-6 | Ng Exercise | Forward placement, resonance |
| 7-8 | Siren | Range, smooth transitions |
| 9-10 | Whisper Sing | Reset, cool down |

**Consistency matters more than duration.** 10 minutes every day for 2 weeks will change your voice more than 2 hours once a month.

## Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### "I can't hit high notes"
**Reality:** You probably can. You're just tensing up. Try the siren exercise — let it crack. The crack means you're at your transition point. Train it, don't avoid it.

### "My voice sounds thin"
**Reality:** You're not placing the sound forward. Do the Ng exercise. Feel the buzz in your cheekbones. That's where your tone should live.

### "I run out of breath quickly"
**Reality:** You're not breathing from your diaphragm. Put your hand on your stomach. Breathe in — stomach goes OUT. Sing — stomach slowly goes IN. If your stomach isn't moving, you're doing it wrong.

### "I've been singing for years but sound the same"
**Reality:** You've been practicing the wrong things consistently. Technique beats talent every time. Get a coach, fix the foundation, then practice.
