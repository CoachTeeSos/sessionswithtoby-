# Daily Content Generation Style Guide & Template
# This ensures every article is consistent, high-quality, and non-hallucinated.

## CONTENT PILLARS (rotate through these daily)

1. **Vocal Technique** — Breathing, placement, range, registers, exercises
2. **Performance Confidence** — Stage fright, presence, audience connection
3. **Voice Science** — How the voice works, anatomy, acoustics
4. **Practical Fixes** — Specific problems (cracks, breathiness, strain)
5. **Industry Insights** — Music industry, gigging, recording, social media
6. **Life Coaching** — Mindset, confidence, personal growth (crossover content)
7. **Student Spotlights** — Transformation stories, testimonials

## ARTICLE TEMPLATE (follow exactly)

```
# [Number] [Problem] — [Benefit]

## Hook (2-3 sentences)
Start with a relatable pain point. Use "you" language.
Example: "You've been singing for years but one note always cracks. You've tried everything. It still happens."

## The Real Cause (1 paragraph)
Explain WHY this happens. Be specific. Use hardware-software framework.
Example: "Your vocal folds are fine — it's the coordination between breath pressure and fold tension that breaks down at that specific pitch."

## The Fix (numbered steps, 3-5 steps)
Each step should be:
- Actionable (do this, not "try to feel this")
- Specific (count numbers, note names, timings)
- Testable (user can verify it worked)

## Exercise Section
- Name of exercise
- Duration
- Step-by-step instructions
- What to feel/hear
- Common mistakes

## CTA
- Link to quiz.html or book.html
- Natural, not salesy
- "Want to know exactly what's holding you back? Take the 30-second quiz."
```

## SEO RULES
- Title must include primary keyword naturally
- H2s should contain related keywords
- Meta description: 150-160 characters, includes keyword, has CTA
- Internal links: at least 2 per article (to home, quiz, book, or other articles)
- Word count: 800-1500 words minimum for daily articles
- Geo articles: 1500-2500 words, comprehensive

## ANTI-HALLUCINATION RULES
- NEVER make up statistics without citing sources
- NEVER claim specific study results unless verifiable
- NEVER invent student names or specific testimonials
- ALWAYS use "many singers", "most vocal coaches agree", "research shows" with general references
- ALWAYS stick to established vocal pedagogy (NYVC, Estill, CVT methods)
- ALWAYS include a disclaimer for medical-adjacent advice
