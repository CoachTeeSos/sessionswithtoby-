The Singer Who Couldn't Finish a Sentence

He could hit any note in his range. His tone was warm, full, technically impressive. But every time he performed, his phrases would die halfway through. He'd run out of air right before the important word — the hook, the climax, the moment that mattered.

"I thought I just needed stronger lungs," he told me. "I tried breathing exercises every day. Nothing changed."

Here's what I told him: Your lungs are fine. Your vocal folds are leaking air. Every time you sing, you're wasting breath because your folds aren't closing efficiently. You're not running out of air — you're spending it badly.

We spent one session working on fold closure. The next day, he sang a full verse without gasping. Same lungs. Same breath. Different coordination.

This is the #2 issue I see. Right after voice cracks. Most singers never connect breath support to fold closure. They're not the same thing.

The "ZH" Exercise (2 Minutes)

This trains your vocal folds to close efficiently — so you stop wasting air and start finishing phrases.

1
Say "ZH" — like the "s" in "measure." Sustain it: "ZZZZHHH." Feel the buzz in your mouth.
2
Put a voice behind it. "VZHH" — like a very exaggerated "V" into a voiced "ZH." Feel the vibration start.
3
Sing "ZHee" on a scale: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1. The "ZH" onset ensures clean fold closure before the note starts.
4
Now sing "See" on the same scale — but keep the same efficient onset. Notice how less air escapes.
2 minutes. Do this 3x daily for 1 week. You'll feel the difference immediately.
