# NYVC Exercise Library
## Source: New York Vocal Coaching (Justin Stoney)

### ONSET EXERCISES

**1. The "Gee" Onset**
- Sing "Gee" (as in "Geese") on a comfortable pitch
- Feel the clean, firm onset — no breathiness, no glottal
- Pattern: 5-4-3-2-1 (descending scale)
- Purpose: Clean vocal fold closure

**2. The "Nay" Onset**
- Sing "Nay" (as in "Nasty") with a bratty, speech-like quality
- Pattern: 1-2-3-4-5 (ascending scale)
- Purpose: Engage twang, forward placement

**3. The "Mum" Sustain**
- Sustain "Mum" on one pitch
- Feel the buzz in the lips, forward resonance
- Hold for 8-10 seconds
- Purpose: Breath efficiency, forward placement

### SUSTAIN EXERCISES

**4. The "Zee" Sustain**
- Sustain "Zee" (as in "Zebra") on a comfortable pitch
- Feel the vibration in the "mask" (cheekbones, nose)
- Hold for 10-15 seconds
- Purpose: Resonance awareness, steady airflow

**5. The "Vvv" Sustain**
- Sustain "Vvv" (as in "Very") with lip buzz
- Feel the connection between breath and tone
- Hold for 8-12 seconds
- Purpose: Breath support coordination

### SCALE EXERCISES

**6. The "Wee" Scale**
- Sing "Wee" (as in "Week") on a 5-tone scale: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1
- Start in chest voice, allow natural transition upward
- Purpose: Registration awareness

**7. The "Nah" Octave**
- Sing "Nah" (as in "Not") on an octave scale: 1-8-1
- Focus on smooth transition through the passaggio
- Purpose: Registration coordination

**8. The "Gug" Arpeggio**
- Sing "Gug" (as in "Gut") on a 1-3-5-8-5-3-1 arpeggio
- Feel the chest voice engagement
- Purpose: Chest voice strength, low-larynx coordination

### AGILITY EXERCISES

**9. The "Koo-Koo-Koo" Run**
- Sing "Koo" (as in "Cool") in a quick 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1 pattern
- Light, quick, speech-like
- Purpose: Vocal fold agility, coordination

**10. The "Nay-Nay-Nay" Triplet**
- Sing "Nay" in triplet patterns: 1-2-3, 2-3-4, 3-4-5
- Bratty, speech-like quality
- Purpose: Twang engagement, agility

### STYLE EXERCISES

**11. The "Cry" Exercise**
- Sing "Wah" (as in "Water") with a cry-like quality
- Feel the "sob" in the voice
- Pattern: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1
- Purpose: Mix voice coordination, emotional connection

**12. The "Belt" Exercise**
- Sing "Hey" (as in "Hey!") with a belt quality
- Start comfortable, gradually increase range
- Purpose: Belt coordination, thick fold engagement

**13. The "Twang" Exercise**
- Sing "Nay" (as in "Nasty") with a nasal, bratty quality
- Feel the "ring" in the voice
- Purpose: Forward projection, cutting power

### RESONANCE EXERCISES

**14. The "Mum-Mum-Mum" Buzz**
- Hum "Mum" on a comfortable pitch
- Feel the buzz in the lips, cheekbones, nose
- Pattern: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1
- Purpose: Forward resonance, "mask" placement

**15. The "Wee-Oh" Slide**
- Slide from "Wee" (high) to "Oh" (low) on a siren
- Feel the resonance shift
- Purpose: Resonance awareness, registration tracking

### PASSAGGIO EXERCISES

**16. The "Gee-Goo" Transition**
- Alternate between "Gee" (closed) and "Goo" (open) on the same pitch
- Feel the vowel modification
- Purpose: Passaggio coordination

**17. The "Nay-Nah" Scale**
- Sing "Nay" going up, "Nah" coming down
- Feel the natural vowel modification
- Purpose: Covering, vowel modification

### DAILY WARM-UP ROUTINE (15-20 min)

1. **Breathing** (2 min) — "Shh" hiss, sustained
2. **Onset** (3 min) — "Gee" and "Nay" onsets
3. **Sustain** (3 min) — "Zee" and "Vvv" sustains
4. **Scales** (5 min) — "Wee" and "Nah" scales
5. **Agility** (3 min) — "Koo" runs
6. **Style** (4 min) — "Cry" and "Belt" exercises
7. **Song Application** (5 min) — Apply to current repertoire

### PRACTICE RULES (Justin Stoney)

1. **Never practice pain** — if it hurts, stop
2. **Slow is fast** — build coordination slowly
3. **Mirror + Recording** — see and hear yourself
4. **Apply immediately** — exercises → songs
5. **Consistency > Duration** — 15 min daily > 2 hours weekly
6. **Hydrate** — water is your vocal cord's best friend
7. **Rest is practice** — vocal folds need recovery
